      ### Layers of the Atmosphere

      The atmosphere is divided into several layers based on temperature and composition:

      1. Troposphere: The lowest layer where weather occurs and where most of the 
      atmospheric mass is found. It extends up to about 8-15 kilometers (5-9 miles) above the
      Earth's surface.
      - Weather: All weather phenomena, such as rain, snow, and storms, occur in the
      troposphere.
      - Airplanes: Most commercial airplanes fly within the lower to middle part of the 
      troposphere.

      2. Stratosphere: Extends from about 15 to 50 kilometers (9 to 31 miles) above the 
      Earth's surface. It contains the ozone layer, which absorbs and scatters ultraviolet solar 
      radiation.
      - Ozone Layer: Protects life on Earth by absorbing most of the sun's harmful UV 
      radiation.
      - Jet Streams: High-altitude, fast-moving winds that influence weather patterns.

      3. Mesosphere: Extends from about 50 to 85 kilometers (31 to 53 miles) above the
      Earth. It is the layer where most meteors burn up upon entering the Earth's atmosphere.
      - Meteor Burning: Meteors entering the atmosphere at high speeds ignite and 
      disintegrate in this layer.
      - Temperature: Temperatures decrease with altitude, making it the coldest layer.

      4. Thermosphere: Extends from about 85 to 600 kilometers (53 to 373 miles) above the
      Earth. It is characterized by high temperatures due to the absorption of high-energy
      radiation.
      - Auroras: The Northern and Southern Lights occur in this layer due to interactions 
      between solar wind and the Earth's magnetic field.
      - International Space Station: Orbits within the thermosphere.

      5. Exosphere: The outermost layer, extending from about 600 kilometers (373 miles) 
      to 10,000 kilometers (6,200 miles) above the Earth. It gradually fades into outer space.
      - Space Boundary: The transition zone between Earth's atmosphere and outer space.
      - Satellites: Many satellites orbit within this layer.

      ### Functions of the Atmosphere

      1. Protection from Radiation: The atmosphere filters harmful solar radiation, 
      including ultraviolet rays, protecting living organisms on Earth.
      2. Temperature Regulation: It helps maintain Earth's temperature through the 
      greenhouse effect, trapping heat and preventing extreme temperature fluctuations.
      3. Oxygen Supply: Provides essential oxygen for respiration in animals and humans.
      4. Weather and Climate: The atmosphere is responsible for weather patterns and climate.
